Natural Medicines Are The Future

Healthcare in our country is inaccessible and unaffordable, and our medicine cannot adequately treat many common ailments including depression, chronic pain, addiction, autoimmune disease and certain cancers.  People are left to take their health into their own hands and seek new ways of healing. Because of this, alternative medicine has become ubiquitous in our mainstream culture. Nutrition and lifestyle movements, such as veganism, keto, and paleo abound. Consumption of herbs and supplements is at an all time high. Juice bars are everywhere, promising healing effects from their tonics. Yoga is now a mainstay of our society. Crystal healing has become so popular that mining is having untoward effects on the environment. Discussions about ayahuasca ceremonies are almost commonplace. We have a growing interest in healing and spirituality in our culture, and people everywhere are waking up.


Healing Ourselves & The Planet


Global awakening is spilling over into environmental awareness and action. Greta Thunberg is now a household name and inspiring youth all over the world to take action. People are eating less meat, going organic, recycling, composting, planting gardens, joining co-ops, reducing plastic waste, riding bicycles, taking public transit, switching to hybrid and electric cars, using solar and other sustainable energies, and making purchases from more socially conscious, ethical companies. Searching for Medicine explores traditional healing practices from around the globe, which often consider the health of the environment as an integral part of our personal wellbeing. They recognize the inextricable link between planetary health and human health, and how we treat the earth is ultimately how we treat ourselves. This perspective is vital in the face of global warming and other threats to our planet.

A New Era Of Female Leadership


Our global society is changing with women leading the way. We have entered a new female led era marked by communal perspective and action. Sixty percent of physicians are female, which is changing the way we receive care. An essential element of Searching for Medicine is finding a new paradigm of healing, shifting away from the current patriarchal system and incorporating a new perspective, one which embraces the feminine. 

Supporting native communities and protecting the medicine


In each episode we visit and learn from healers and communities who generously share their sacred wisdom for the benefit of all. It is our intention to transmit this knowledge ethically and with integrity while supporting and giving back to these communities. We are continually assessing our alignment with this mission and are forming a board of cultural advisors.


  • A large portion of our budget is a contribution to the communities we visit, and the healers we work with are the highest paid members of the team for each episode.


  • Products are sourced directly from native communities when possible.


  • Donations go towards natural medicine education.
